Methodology

How we do the
impossible
on a student
budget

01
Ancient DNA Acquisition & Assembly

We source aDNA from publicly available fossil specimen databases, museum collections, and published genomic studies. Custom bioinformatic pipelines handle damage pattern correction, de novo assembly, and quality filtering specific to degraded ancient samples — making high-quality ancient genome work accessible without institutional sequencing resources.

02
Comparative Genomic Analysis

Extinct genomes are mapped against their closest living relatives to identify divergent loci responsible for species-specific traits — morphology, physiology, ecological adaptation. This defines the target list for potential re-expression and forms the scientific foundation for any de-extinction program.

03
CRISPR Candidate Design

For viable targets, we design guide RNA candidates for each locus using our open-source gRNA design tools — the same pipeline behind our wolf parvovirus resistance work, now generalized for extinction-reversal targets. All designs are published for community review and validation.

04
Open Publication — No Exceptions

Every pipeline, dataset, and result is published openly. We believe de-extinction science moves faster when it's shared, and that no single lab — regardless of funding — should hold a monopoly on bringing species back. The genomes of the lost belong to everyone.

Our mission

De-extinction is too important to leave to one lab

Rewild Genomics exists to build the open-source genomic infrastructure for species revival — and to prove that rigorous de-extinction research doesn't require a nine-figure budget or a corporate lab. It requires the right questions, the right tools, and the willingness to publish everything.

01

De-extinction Science

aDNA pipelines and CRISPR frameworks for ancient genome reconstruction and phenotypic revival of extinct North American species.

02

Genomic Tooling

Open-source tools for CRISPR design, population genetics, and disease genomics applied directly to conservation and de-extinction targets.

03

Conservation Policy

Translating genomic research into actionable support for reintroduction programs, habitat protection, and ecological restoration in the west.

04

Radical Openness

Every pipeline, dataset, and result is publicly available and peer-reviewable. No IP walls. No exclusivity. The genomes of the lost belong to everyone.
